What ships in Phase 1.

Five services that land first. Each one targets a specific use case in agent commerce, has a working price assumption, and a target volume that informs the cost model. None are live yet. The schedule below is the published intent, not a production offer.

V-200 · What's included

What you get with every service.

  • A signed response from the platform's published key, allowing independent verification that the response originated from Paxiom.
  • A permanent audit record written to AO/Arweave, retrievable by any party for the life of the network.
  • The verification artifact (ZK proof, attestation, simulation receipt) in the format specified for the service.
  • x402-native payment flow with no API key provisioning, no contract negotiation, and no relationship requirement.
V-300 · What's not included

What the platform doesn't do.

  • No custody of customer assets. The platform holds no wallets, balances, or tokens belonging to customers.
  • No SLA in v1. Service level commitments will be added as Phase 1 services accumulate operational track record.
  • No enterprise features (single sign-on, dedicated tenancy, custom retention) at the launch tier. These belong to later phases.
  • No chargebacks or dispute resolution. Settlement is final at the protocol level.
  • No off-x402 billing rails in v1. Customers pay through standard x402 flows; invoiced billing is a Phase 2+ consideration for B2B contracts.
V-400 · Beyond Phase 1

What's coming, but not yet.

Phase 2 expands into ZK proofs of computation on private data — HIPAA-compliant medical workflows, financial-data verification with regulatory transparency. Phase 3 adds verified AI inference. Phase 4 extends into IoT telemetry. Phase 5 completes the migration to post-quantum proving systems.

None of these are part of the schedule above. Each comes online in its own phase, with its own pricing model, on its own timeline. The Project Narrative covers the longer arc; this page covers what ships first.
