What Proofs Let You Do

Paxiom feeds are designed for teams that already act on on-chain data but want stronger guarantees than a raw RPC response, subgraph, or hosted indexer can provide.

Why It Matters

Most market infrastructure treats Ethereum state as something a service provider reports. Paxiom treats it as something a subscriber can verify, store, and compare across time.

That creates a stronger primitive for arbitrage monitoring, liquidation readiness, risk controls, bridge checks, and any workflow where the cost of trusting stale or incorrect state is higher than the cost of verifying a proof-backed record.

For trading desksVerified pool state can gate signals before capital moves.
For risk systemsState facts arrive with proof context and commitment lookup.
For protocolsExternal monitors can consume facts without giving Paxiom custody.
For future automationThe same records become inputs to AO and recursive proof workflows.

Where The Roadmap Goes

The feed starts with real Ethereum storage proof verification and grows toward a broader proof-native coordination layer. Subscribers do not need to wait for the full end state: each stage adds a stricter guarantee to the same data surface.

Nowmpt_verified feeds for Uniswap V3 pool state and contract storage facts.
NextMore predicates: pool reserves, oracle values, lending health inputs, liquidation eligibility, event/log inclusion.
AO committedFacts are submitted to an AO proof-corpus process for durable public coordination.
Recursive historySegments aggregate toward a genesis-to-present Ethereum corpus.
Beaconless proof layerConsensus and execution proofs reduce reliance on trusted checkpoints and oracle-style beacons.

Subscriber Access

Production access is authenticated and provisioned per subscriber. Scopes can be limited by predicate, feed, or subject so early customers only receive the feeds they are approved to use. 

GET /health
GET /v1/feed/latest
GET /v1/feed/subjects
GET /v1/feed/commitment/:commitment

Paxiom data feeds are not custody, trade execution, or investment advice. They are proof-labeled records that downstream systems can verify and consume according to their own risk policy. API keys are required for live production feeds.
