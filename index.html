Why It Matters

Most market infrastructure treats Ethereum state as something a service provider reports. Paxiom treats it as something a subscriber can verify, store, and compare across time.

That creates a stronger primitive for arbitrage monitoring, liquidation readiness, risk controls, bridge checks, and any workflow where the cost of trusting stale or incorrect state is higher than the cost of verifying a proof-backed record.

What Paxiom Is Not

Paxiom is not a low-latency price oracle, a subgraph replacement, or a hosted archive node. It is a verification layer for Ethereum facts: state-root-bound records, proof context, commitment lookup, and a path toward recursive proof depth.

Not Pyth or Chainlink

Paxiom does not begin by reporting prices into contracts. It begins by proving what Ethereum state said.

Not a subgraph

Indexers optimize queryability. Paxiom optimizes evidence that a specific fact opened under a specific root.

Not your node team

Running Erigon gives control. Paxiom gives productized proof records and subscriber-grade commitments.
