What is being built

A verification layer, not a payment rail.

The agent economy needs a substrate where machines can transact across chains, services, and trust boundaries without relying on any single intermediary's honesty. Paxiom is one specific bet on what part of that substrate should look like — proof services, audit relay mechanics, identity-bound service access — built on permanent autonomous compute and zero-knowledge verification.

The drawing set goes deeper. The Project Narrative explains the territory and the bet. The Phase 1 Blueprint details the five services that ship first. The Operations Runbook covers what happens on the days things go wrong, and the days they don't.

Why this exists

The proof is the point.

Most agent infrastructure being built today asks the consumer to trust the operator. Paxiom is built on the opposite assumption: every output the platform produces is independently verifiable, and the audit trail is permanent rather than retained at the operator's discretion.

This is a slower way to build. It requires substrate decisions — HyperBEAM compute, AO/Arweave audit, archive-anchored state reconstruction — that competitors using commodity infrastructure don't make. The bet is that as trust failures accumulate in adjacent agent service categories, the customers who care about verifiable outputs will be the ones who matter most.
